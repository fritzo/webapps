The Rational Keyboard

Fritz Obermeyer

The Rational Keyboard is an experiment attemtpting to overcome some of the flaws in the piano keyboard layout and the equal temperment 12-tone scale.

Let us begin with a psychoacoustic model of harmony and consonance. Given that the ear uses both place-coding and temporal coding of pitch, and that temporal coding is used to associate partials of a tone, we postulate a scalar quantity consonance which which is monotone decreasing in the complexity of a temporally coded signal. One model of signal complexity in a population of coupled oscillators (such as neurons in the efferent fibers leaving the cochlea) is the average coupling energy in the population.

... We approximate the Arnold tongue scenario with a simple quadratic energy function on the activation vector over frequencies. Let QQ be the set of positive rational numbers, each expressed as m/n for relatively prime integers m,n. We endow QQ with the following pseudometric: Suppose p,q:QQ, and p/q = m/n for relatively prime m,n. then d(p,q) = m^2 + n^2. Then d(-,-) is symmetric and obeys the triangle inequality.
